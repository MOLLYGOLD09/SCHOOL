📚 School History

A journey of growth and excellence in education

Silangan National High School was established through the dedicated effort of Mrs. Carolina D. Tumuran, then a Barangay Captain and PTA officer of Silangan Elementary School. She filed a "Parents' Resolution" requesting that a parcel of land from the RSBS/AFP (Retirement and Benefit System of the Armed Forces of the Philippines) be donated to establish the school.

The school began as an annex of San Mateo National High School under the leadership of Miss Juana M. Garovillas, Principal In-Charge from 1994-1997, with coordination from DECS officials.

The first operation of this school was housed at the Old Health Center of the AFP Village, a small makeshift building located in the lot area, which served as the school's home for four years from 1994-1997. The first two years of operation were handled by Mrs. Zenaida D. Corpuz, a lone teacher residing at the AFP Village, holding an extension position in the plantilla of San Mateo National High School.

Mr. Remegio B. Olesco was locally designated as O.I.C. from 1994-1997. On June 23, 1998, he was appointed and officially designated Teacher In-Charge by DECS Regional Office and Division Office under Special Order No.30 series of 1998 up to November 2003. Mr. Ricardo Vergara then served as school head from 2003 until 2013.

Following Mr. Vergara, Silangan National High School was headed by Mr. Alfredo D. Lopez, Principal III. During this period, the school had 78 nationally-funded teachers for School Year 2016, with 25 male and 53 female teachers.

👩‍🎓 Principal's Message

Leadership with a vision for excellence

Mrs. Susana J. Sacatrapos, Principal IV, presently heads Silangan National High School. She brings with her a wealth of experience and dedication to educational leadership.

Before her appointment here, she served as Principal of San Isidro National High School and San Isidro Senior High School for 4 years. She was then transferred to Tagumpay National High School, a very performing school in Tagumpay, Montalban, Rizal, where she made significant contributions to academic excellence.

In July 2024, she was transferred to Silangan National High School, bringing her vision and expertise to our institution. Under her leadership, the school continues to grow and excel in providing quality education to the community.

Currently, the school has 78 nationally-funded teachers and a total student population of 2,927 for School Year 2024-2025. Our dedicated faculty consists of 25 male and 53 female teachers, serving 1,538 male and 1,389 female students.

"We are committed to nurturing every student's potential and preparing them to become responsible, productive members of society. Together, we will continue to strive for excellence in education."

🏃 Our Mission & Vision

Guiding principles that shape our educational approach

Our Mission

We dream of Filipinos who passionately love their country and whose values and competencies enable them to realize their full potential and contribute meaningfully to building the nation.

As a learner-centered public institution, the Department of Education continuously improves itself to better serve its stakeholders.

Our Vision

To protect and promote the right of every Filipino to quality, equitable, culture-based, and complete basic education where:

  • Students learn in a child-friendly, gender-sensitive, safe, and motivating environment.
  • Teachers facilitate learning and constantly nurture every learner.
  • Administrators and staff, as stewards of the institution, ensure an enabling and supportive environment for effective learning to happen.
  • Family, community, and other stakeholders are actively engaged and share responsibility for developing life-long learners.
